Rural patients get virtual access to 35+ specialties without leaving home. When a procedure or in-person care is needed, their RHH specialist coordinates directly with UMC through connected care — then the patient returns to the same specialists. One network, one continuous relationship.

How the Hub works

Any provider with excess capacity can be placed on the Hub to see rural patients remotely — filling their patient census with new volume at no added staffing cost. Where a provider or department is already at capacity, Rural Health Hub supplies its own specialists for outpatient long-term management, then connects directly into the hospital system — so either way, care flows through one connected network.

Raising the Bar in Rural Texas

Better care, closer to home

This partnership builds a single connected network across rural West Texas. RHH tele-specialists manage patients virtually in their home community across 35+ specialties; when an intervention is truly needed, a specialist-to-specialist referral sends them to UMC and Texas Tech for the procedure, then discharges them back to the same local team for follow-up. The result: patients get specialty care sooner and closer to home, chronic disease is caught and managed earlier, avoidable ER visits and readmissions fall, and rural hospitals keep volume and revenue in their own communities.

Specialty care reaches the community directly by telehealth — so patients no longer drive hours or go without. When in-person visits, procedures, or surgeries are needed, they are handled at UMC.

When patients can reach a specialist locally, the emergency room stops being the default — cutting avoidable visits and easing strain on both rural hospital and UMC emergency departments.

Conditions are caught and managed sooner — improving outcomes and keeping small problems from becoming emergencies.
