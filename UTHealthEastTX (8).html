Strategic Partnership Proposal

Coordinated care that reaches rural communities.

A connected network across rural East Texas: RHH tele-specialists manage patients close to home, and when in-person care is needed, a specialist-to-specialist referral makes the connection to UT Health East Texas simple and seamless — then returns the patient to the same team upon discharge for coordinated follow-up.

The Need for Coordinated Care

Rural communities need specialty care that’s coordinated — not fragmented.

When specialty care is hard to reach, rural patients face delays, fragmented hand-offs, and long drives — and when in-person care is truly needed, the connection to a regional hospital is too often left to chance. A coordinated network changes that: care is managed locally, and the path to UT Health East Texas is streamlined and simple when it’s required — with fewer avoidable readmissions along the way.

Distance & workforce gaps

Specialist shortages and long travel mean rural patients defer care — or seek it elsewhere — until an intervention is needed.

Fragmented hand-offs

Without a coordinated path, referrals stall, follow-ups slip, and patients fall through the gaps between local care and the specialist they need.

Avoidable readmissions

Patients discharged without coordinated local follow-up come back. Returning them to the managing specialist closes the loop and cuts 30-day readmissions.

The premise is simple: coordinated specialty care, delivered locally and connected upward only when in-person treatment is needed. Embedding RHH specialists in the community extends UT Health East Texas’s reach to more rural patients — without recruiting or hiring — while keeping continuity of care intact from the first visit through discharge.

Excess capacity, none of the overhead

UT Health East Texas providers with open capacity serve as the tele-specialty providers for rural communities directly. The Rural Health Hub supplements only when there’s no availability on your side — filling gaps rather than competing for them. In effect, this puts your system’s spare specialist capacity to work across rural Texas — generating new encounters and revenue without the burden of recruiting, hiring, and salarying fractional specialists.

Patient Health

Healthier patients, cared for closer to home.

The clearest beneficiary of a coordinated network is the patient. When specialty care is managed locally — and connected upward only when in-person treatment is truly needed — rural patients are seen sooner, treated closer to home, and spared the disruptions that come with fragmented care, from long drives to avoidable trips to the emergency room.

Care that stays close to home

Local access to specialty care means patients are managed in their own community, in a relationship they trust — without long drives for every consult and follow-up. Care that’s easier to reach is care patients are more likely to keep up with.

Fewer trips to the ER

When patients can reach a specialist early — or a local provider can get a quick curbside answer — problems are caught and managed before they escalate. That heads off avoidable emergency visits that are stressful, disruptive, and often preventable with timely access.

Fewer unnecessary hospital stays

Conditions caught and managed earlier are less likely to require an admission that better access could have prevented. And when a patient is discharged, returning them to the same local team for coordinated follow-up helps keep them from coming back.

Partnership

Extend coordinated care without adding infrastructure or staff.

Partnering with the Rural Health Hub extends UT Health East Texas’s capabilities into rural communities — strengthening connected care and reducing readmissions — without recruiting or hiring additional providers. The need is already there; the network simply makes the connection to your team simple and reliable.

Value for UT Health East Texas

Connected referrals, fewer dropped hand-offs.

Because patients arrive already under active management by their RHH specialist, the hand-off is a true specialist-to-specialist referral — so the patients who reach UT Health East Texas are the ones who genuinely need in-person care, and they arrive prepared, with continuity intact on both sides.
