Strategic Partnership Proposal

Coordinated care that reaches rural communities.

A connected network across rural West Texas: RHH tele-specialists manage patients close to home, and when in-person care is needed, a specialist-to-specialist referral makes the connection to UMC and Texas Tech simple and seamless — then returns the patient to the same team upon discharge for coordinated follow-up.

The Need for Coordinated Care

Rural communities need specialty care that’s coordinated — not fragmented.

When specialty care is hard to reach, rural patients face delays, fragmented hand-offs, and long drives — and when in-person care is truly needed, the connection to a regional hospital is too often left to chance. A coordinated network changes that: care is managed locally, and the path to UMC and Texas Tech is streamlined and simple when it’s required — with fewer avoidable readmissions along the way.

Distance & workforce gaps

Specialist shortages and long travel mean rural patients defer care — or seek it elsewhere — until an intervention is needed.

Fragmented hand-offs

Without a coordinated path, referrals stall, follow-ups slip, and patients fall through the gaps between local care and the specialist they need.

Avoidable readmissions

Patients discharged without coordinated local follow-up come back. Returning them to the managing specialist closes the loop and cuts 30-day readmissions.

The premise is simple: coordinated specialty care, delivered locally and connected upward only when in-person treatment is needed. Embedding RHH specialists in the community extends UMC and Texas Tech’s reach to more rural patients — without recruiting or hiring — while keeping continuity of care intact from the first visit through discharge.

Partnership

Extend coordinated care without adding infrastructure or staff.

Partnering with the Rural Health Hub extends UMC and Texas Tech’s capabilities into rural communities — strengthening connected care and reducing readmissions — without recruiting or hiring additional providers. The need is already there; the network simply makes the connection to your team simple and reliable.

Value for UMC · Texas Tech

Connected referrals, fewer dropped hand-offs.

Because patients arrive already under active management by their RHH specialist, the hand-off is a true specialist-to-specialist referral — so the patients who reach UMC are the ones who genuinely need in-person care, and they arrive prepared, with continuity intact on both sides.

  • A coordinated connection to rural patients
  • In-person care delivered when it’s truly needed
  • Specialist-to-specialist continuity of care
  • Reduced readmissions via coordinated follow-up
  • Rural-health research opportunities
